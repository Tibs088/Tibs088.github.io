Matthew Hartley

Junior Software Developer/Aspiring Developer and Games Designer

I like to think of myself as someone who can find the fun in what I do. Coming from care work, turning the average day into something to enjoy was always important to help those I was working with to increase their motivation. Care work also gave me solid experience in communicating with others, since a lot of the people I was working with were non-verbal or had limited communication, I had to learn to adjust my communication both spoken and not, to become a good carer. This was particularly helpful for me having grown up diagnosed with Autism.


In tech I have been inspired by video games, originally going to university to study games design but shifting into the direction of software, since I found the fun in simply making computers do things. However, that games design experience isn’t wasted. Since a lot of games design is focused around making the players' experience as exciting as possible, I’m constantly conscious of the user experience. In games that would be ensuring that game mechanics were accessible and controls were intuitive, which lines up with software in making apps user friendly and readable.

Tech Skills and Experience

On this course I developed a keen interest in React and Frontend development, building a fullstack application using agile development methods.

While on this course I took part in game jams and completed several large scale assignments and projects.
